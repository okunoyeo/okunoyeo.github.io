Good Enough is Enough

An aspect I struggle with in both coding and any other project I work on is allowing myself to submit work that’s not quite perfect. I seem to get stuck on the small details and trying to follow a certain sequence in order to complete a project. What ends up happening is something akin to this: Where I start off strongly and add as much details as I can, then as a deadline approaches, I am forced to complete the project in a way that is less than desirable. When in reality if I had tried to get a comprehensive view of the project and complete a rough sketch then get the more specific detail done it would get to a “good enough” stage without any sloppy sections.

Following the same vein, I like to try to accomplish as much as requested, and if I have a client asking me to perform a task I would want to do as much as possible. But I need to be able to give a more realistic view of what can be done in a certain amount of time, with the resources I have. I as the programmer would need to tell the client what pros and cons exist for different options, while listening to their input.
